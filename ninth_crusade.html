Ninth Crusade | 1271-1272

Beginning

Led by Edward I of England and often grouped with the Eighth Crusade, this crusade started when Prince Edward I of England decided to keep crusading after the end of the Eighth Crusade.

Journey

Edward, with only 1000 men and a few French knights, traveled to Cyprus and from there to Acre, arriving in September 1271. He was able to take the city of Nazareth but was unable to secure much more than that. In 1272, Edward signed a truce with the Mamluks and started his journey home.

After

In the time between the end of the Ninth Crusade, and the end of the crusades, the Muslims took more and more territory from the Crusader States, eventually dimishing them to just four cities. These last four cities, Tyre, Sidion, Acre, and Beirut, fell in 1291. Cyprus would remain in Christian hands until the 16th ecentury when it was taken over by the Turks.

Legacy

This crusade, considered the last noteworthy crusade to the holy lands, accomplished almost nothing.
